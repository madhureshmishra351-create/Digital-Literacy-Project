#  Selected Cybercrime Type

I chose **UPI / Online Payment Fraud** because it affects almost everyone today. Most of us regularly use digital payment applications such as **Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm** for daily transactions. These apps make payments fast and convenient, but unfortunately this convenience is also what fraudsters take advantage of. People trust these applications, and scammers know this well.

#  Case Study Description

UPI fraud is very sneaky and usually begins with a phone call from someone who sounds genuine and trustworthy. The caller often speaks calmly and professionally, pretending to be a bank representative or customer support agent. They may claim that there is a problem with your bank account, or that you are eligible for a refund or reward. Whatever story they use, their main goal is always the same: they want you to share your **OTP** or approve a payment request.

One reason UPI fraud is so effective is that scammers create a strong sense of urgency. For example, they may say that your account will be blocked within ten minutes unless you act immediately. In such situations, people often panic and stop thinking carefully. They react quickly without verifying the information, and that is exactly when the scam happens. Once the payment is approved, the money is transferred instantly and it is very difficult to recover.

Anyone can become a victim of this type of fraud. Students, working professionals, and even experienced people can fall for it if they are caught off guard. When such fraud happens, people not only lose money but also feel embarrassed and lose trust in others.

The best way to protect yourself from UPI fraud is actually very simple: **never share your OTP or UPI PIN with anyone**. Genuine banks and companies will never ask for this information. If something feels suspicious or unusual, pause for a moment and think carefully before taking any action. That uneasy feeling often exists for a reason, and trusting it can help protect you from becoming a victim of fraud.
